4 Using ChatGPT for Legal Research: Step-by-Step Process

How to Frame Your Legal Questions Clearly

The quality of the output you receive from ChatGPT depends greatly on how clearly and
precisely you phrase your questions or prompts. Here are some tips for framing your legal
questions effectively:

  • Be Specific: Instead of asking a vague question like "Tell me about contract law," ask "What
    are the essential elements of a valid contract under Indian Contract Act, 1872?"
  • Provide Context: Briefly mention relevant facts or legal provisions to help ChatGPT
    understand the background. For example, "Explain the concept of 'consideration' in a
    contract involving sale of goods in India."
  • Use Simple Language: Avoid overly complex sentences. Write as if explaining to a junior
    lawyer or a law student.
  • Break Down Complex Questions: If your legal query is complicated, split it into smaller parts
    or steps.
  • Ask for Examples: To understand difficult concepts better, request examples relevant to
    Indian law.

Using ChatGPT to Understand Legal Concepts

One of ChatGPT's strengths is simplifying complex legal ideas. You can ask ChatGPT to
explain concepts such as "What is 'Doctrine of Promissory Estoppel' in Indian law?" or "How
does the Indian Penal Code define 'criminal intimidation'?" The Al will provide you with a
clear, concise explanation which you can then verify with authoritative texts.

This feature helps lawyers grasp unfamiliar topics quickly or refresh their memory without
digging through lengthy textbooks or multiple websites.

Drafting Case Summaries

Case law plays a vital role in legal research. ChatGPT can help draft summaries of important
court judgments once you provide the case name and basic facts. For example, you might
input:

"Summarize the Supreme Court judgment in Kesavananda Bharati v. State of Kerala
focusing on the 'basic structure doctrine'."

The Al will produce a brief, structured summary highlighting key points, which you can then
review and refine as needed.

Creating Legal Opinions

Drafting legal opinions often involves analyzing facts and law to provide reasoned advice.
While ChatGPT cannot replace your legal judgment, it can assist in generating a draft outline
or initial opinion based on facts you provide. For instance:

"Draft a legal opinion on the enforceability of oral contracts under Indian law."

ChatGPT will generate a structured opinion including relevant legal principles, which you can
adapt and expand.

Building Checklists and Outlines

For procedural or compliance tasks, lawyers often use checklists or outlines. ChatGPT can
quickly create these based on your requirements. For example:

"Create a checklist for due diligence when drafting a property sale agreement in India."
This helps lawyers ensure they do not miss any important steps or documents.

Drafting Basic Legal Templates

ChatGPT can help draft basic templates such as notices, affidavits, or contracts by following
your instructions. You can prompt:

"Draft a simple rental agreement template under Indian law."

Remember, templates generated by ChatGPT should always be reviewed carefully and
customized to specific cases.

Practical Prompts for Each Step

To get the best results from ChatGPT, here are some example prompts you can use for each
legal research task:

  • Understanding Concepts: "Explain the concept of 'consideration' under the Indian Contract
    Act in simple terms."
  • Case Summaries: "Provide a summary of the Supreme Court's ruling in Maneka Gandhi v.
    Union of India."
  • Legal Opinions: "Draft a preliminary legal opinion on the legality of digital signatures in
    India."
  • Checklists: "List the documents required for company registration under the Companies Act,
    2013."
  • Templates: "Create a basic non-disclosure agreement template for use in India."

Using ChatGPT in this step-by-step way can make your legal research faster and more
efficient while ensuring accuracy and clarity. Remember, Al is your assistant - always apply
your legal knowledge to verify and improve the Al's output.

5 Sample Prompts for Indian Legal Research

Contract Law

Contract law is fundamental for most lawyers. Use ChatGPT to clarify concepts, draft
clauses, or understand recent case law. Sample prompts:

"Explain the essential elements of a valid contract under the Indian Contract Act, 1872."

"Draft a clause for force majeure in a commercial contract governed by Indian law."

"Summarize the Supreme Court's decision in Hadley v. Baxendale regarding damages for
breach of contract."

"What are the remedies available under the Indian Contract Act for breach of contract?"

Criminal Law

In criminal practice, ChatGPT can assist with legal definitions, procedural steps, or case
summaries. Sample prompts:

"Define the offence of 'cheating' under the Indian Penal Code."

"Explain the procedure for filing a First Information Report (FIR) in India."

"Summarize the key points in the landmark judgment of D.K. Basu v. State of West Bengal
on police custody."

"Draft a bail application template for a non-bailable offence under Indian law."

Company Law

Company law involves complex statutory provisions. ChatGPT can help simplify rules or
draft compliance checklists. Sample prompts:

"List the main requirements for incorporation of a private limited company under the
Companies Act, 2013."

"Explain the duties of directors under Indian company law."

"Create a checklist for annual compliance for a company registered in India."

"Draft a basic shareholders' agreement template for an Indian company."

Family Law

Family law covers sensitive and diverse matters. ChatGPT can help draft petitions, explain
laws, or prepare checklists. Sample prompts:

"Explain the grounds for divorce under the Hindu Marriage Act, 1955."

"Draft a simple child custody agreement in accordance with Indian law."

"Summarize the key provisions of the Muslim Personal Law regarding inheritance."

"What is the process for adoption under the Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of
Children) Act, 2015?"

Labour Law

Labour law requires knowledge of statutes and compliance. Use ChatGPT for explanations,
draft notices, or checklists. Sample prompts:

"Explain the concept of 'wages' under the Payment of Wages Act, 1936."

"Draft a termination notice for an employee under Indian labour law."

"List the compliances required under the Employees' Provident Fund and Miscellaneous
Provisions Act, 1952."

"What are the legal provisions relating to maternity benefits in India?"

Property Law

Property law involves transactions and disputes over immovable property. ChatGPT can
assist with document drafting and concept explanations. Sample prompts:

"Explain the concept of 'title' and 'possession' under Indian property law."

"Draft a sale deed template for immovable property in India."

"Summarize the key points in the K.T. Plantation case relating to property rights."

"What are the steps involved in registering a property sale in India?"

How to Adapt Prompts for Your Practice

Every legal practice is unique. To tailor ChatGPT prompts:

  • Add Specific Facts: Include details relevant to your case or client situation.
  • Mention Applicable Laws: Specify the jurisdiction or statute you want ChatGPT to focus on.
  • Request Output Format: Ask for bullet points, summaries, or detailed explanations as
    needed.
  • Iterate and Refine: Use ChatGPT's responses as a draft and ask follow-up questions for
    clarity or expansion.

By mastering prompt creation, you unlock ChatGPT's full potential as a research assistant

6 Best Practices for Lawyers Using ChatGPT

Tips for Accuracy and Reliability

  • Cross-Verify Information: Always check Al-generated answers against authoritative sources
    such as official statutes, government websites, and recognized legal databases like SCC
    Online or Manupatra.
  • Update Knowledge: Remember that ChatGPT's knowledge has a cutoff date (currently 2021
    for most models), so verify if any recent legal developments or amendments have
    occurred.
  • Use Clear, Specific Prompts: The clearer your questions, the more accurate and relevant
    ChatGPT's responses will be.
  • Consult Subject Matter Experts: For specialized or complex topics, confirm Al outputs with
    senior lawyers or experts.
  • Avoid Blind Reliance: Treat ChatGPT as an assistant that provides suggestions, not final
    legal advice.

Avoiding Common Mistakes

  • Don't Share Confidential Client Information: Avoid inputting sensitive data into ChatGPT as
    Al platforms may store or process information externally.
  • Avoid Over-Reliance on Al: Never use ChatGPT to replace your legal reasoning or
    judgment.
  • Beware of Outdated or Incomplete Data: Al may omit recent case law, statutory changes, or
    jurisdiction-specific nuances.
  • Be Mindful of Bias: Al models may reflect biases present in training data, so critically
    evaluate all outputs.
  • Clarify Ambiguities: If ChatGPT's answers seem vague or confusing, rephrase your query or
    ask for
    clarification.

How to Cross-Check AI-Generated Information

  • Use legal databases (SCC Online, Manupatra, Indian Kanoon).
  • Verify citations, case laws, and sections before using them.
  • Refer to Bare Acts for exact wording.
  • Discuss complex cases with senior lawyers or colleagues for additional insights.
  • Keep a habit of updating legal knowledge—AI’s knowledge may lag behind new laws or
    judgments.

Maintaining Professionalism and Ethical Standards

  • Disclose Use of AI When Necessary: If using AI-assisted work products in client
    communications or court submissions, disclose appropriately.
  • Keep Client Interests Paramount: Use AI only to enhance your service quality, not to cut
    corners.
  • Stay Informed About AI Developments: Keep up with emerging AI regulations and guidelines
    affecting legal practice in India.

By following these best practices, Indian lawyers can harness the power of ChatGPT
responsibly and effectively, enhancing productivity without compromising on quality or ethics.
